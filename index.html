My interests center on the interplay between random walks, electrical networks, and Gaussian fields — the circle of ideas connecting discrete potential theory, the discrete Gaussian free field, and the extremes of log-correlated processes.

Research interests

I am drawn to the parts of probability where analysis, combinatorics, and mathematical physics meet. A recurring theme in my reading is a single object — the Green function — seen at once as the occupation time of a random walk, the inverse of a discrete Laplacian, and the covariance of a Gaussian free field. Much of my recent work has been learning how this dictionary ties effective resistance, the discrete Gaussian free field, and branching random walks into one connected set of tools.

  • Random walks and electrical networks
  • Green functions and discrete potential theory
  • The discrete Gaussian free field
  • Branching random walk and log-correlated extremes
  • Large deviations
