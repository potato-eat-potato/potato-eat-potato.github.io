Object: Equilibrium potential

One-Sentence Summary

\(h_A(x) = \mathbb{P}_x(\tau_A < \tau_{D^c})\) — the harmonic function that interpolates between “on the set” and “on the boundary,” whose Dirichlet energy is the capacity of the set.

Definition

For \(A \subset D\): \(h_A(x) = \mathbb{P}_x(\tau_A < \tau_{D^c})\). Harmonic on \(D \setminus A\), \(\equiv 1\) on \(A\), \(\equiv 0\) outside \(D\). Electrically: the voltage when \(A\) is held at \(1\)V and \(D^c\) grounded.

Capacity

\(\mathrm{cap}_D(A) := \mathcal{E}(h_A) = C_{\mathrm{eff}}(A \leftrightarrow D^c)\) — the effective conductance between the set and the boundary (Dirichlet principle: \(h_A\) minimizes energy among its boundary data). Capacity is the right “probabilistic size” of a set: hitting probabilities of small sets scale with capacity, not cardinality or diameter alone.

Why It Matters

Whenever I ask “does the walk hit this set before leaving?” the answer is an equilibrium potential. Last-exit decompositions express \(h_A\) as a Green-function average against an equilibrium measure on \(A\) — this is the standard route to “hitting probability ≈ Green factor × capacity” estimates.

Why This Object Is Central to My Probability Route

The mesoscopic-hole and harmonic-measure questions I want to attack reduce to: equilibrium potential of a small set inside a box. Also: conditioning the DGFF on a set (punctured box, version with sets) produces conditional variances expressible through these same objects.

Tools That Use This Object

  • Turn hitting probability into harmonic function
  • Use effective resistance to control random walk
  • Compare domains by monotonicity

Important Properties

  • \(0 \le h_A \le 1\) with equality regions exactly \(A\) and \(D^c\) (Maximum principle).
  • Monotone in \(A\) and in \(D\).

Where I Encountered This

Voltage problems in Doyle–Snell; named and systematized when I started formulating the hole questions.
