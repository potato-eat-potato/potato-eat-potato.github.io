Object: DGFF

One-Sentence Summary

The Discrete Gaussian Free Field is “the Gaussian measure whose energy is Dirichlet energy” — a random height function whose entire probabilistic structure is encoded in the Green function of a random walk.

Definition

For finite \(D \subset \mathbb{Z}^d\) with zero boundary conditions: the centered Gaussian field \((\varphi_x)_{x \in D}\) with density \[\propto \exp\Big(-\tfrac{1}{4d}\sum_{x \sim y}(\varphi_x - \varphi_y)^2\Big) \quad (\text{ordered pairs}),\] equivalently the centered Gaussian with covariance \(\mathbb{E}[\varphi_x\varphi_y] = G_D(x,y)\), the Killed Green function. Three constructions give the same law — (A) the Gibbs/energy density above, (B) the centered Gaussian with covariance \(G_D = L^{-1}\), and (C) a Hilbert-space Gaussian series in the Dirichlet inner product; I wrote out the A\(=\)B equivalence with proofs in DGFF — definitions and proofs, with a longer conceptual companion in DGFF Lecture 1 — three faces.

This note is deliberately a map, not a textbook chapter — the real content lives in the tool notes linked below.

The Facts I Organize Everything Around

  • Covariance = Green function — so all field estimates reduce to walk estimates: Express DGFF covariance by Green function.
  • Domain Markov property: condition on values outside \(U\); inside you get (independent zero-boundary DGFF on \(U\)) + (harmonic extension of the conditioned values): Use Gaussian conditioning as Markov property.
  • Log-correlated in \(d=2\): \(\mathbb{E}[\varphi_x\varphi_y] \approx \frac{2}{\pi}\log\frac{N}{|x-y|}\) in a box of side \(N\); variance \(\frac{2}{\pi}\log N + O(1)\). No pointwise infinite-volume limit exists in \(d=2\) — hence Regularize infinite graph by finite box.
  • Hierarchical structure: across dyadic scales the field looks like a Branching random walk — the engine of Use multiscale decomposition.
  • Extremes: in \(d=2\), \(\max_{V_N} \varphi = 2\sqrt{g}\,\log N - \tfrac{3}{4}\sqrt{g}\,\log\log N + O_P(1)\) with \(g = 2/\pi\) (Bramson–Ding–Zeitouni). The \(\tfrac34\) (vs. \(\tfrac14\) for i.i.d.) is the fingerprint of the branching structure.
