Theorem

Theorem: Gaussian conditioning formula

Statement

Let \((X, Y)\) be jointly Gaussian (centered, block covariances \(\Sigma_{XX}, \Sigma_{XY}, \Sigma_{YY}\)). Then conditionally on \(Y\): \[X \mid Y \;\sim\; N\Big(\Sigma_{XY}\Sigma_{YY}^{-1} Y,\;\; \Sigma_{XX} - \Sigma_{XY}\Sigma_{YY}^{-1}\Sigma_{YX}\Big).\]

Two structural miracles: the conditional mean is linear in \(Y\), and the conditional covariance is deterministic — it does not depend on the observed values.

Intuition

For Gaussians, conditioning is orthogonal projection in \(L^2\): the conditional mean is the projection of \(X\) onto \(\mathrm{span}(Y)\), the conditional covariance is the squared norm of what’s left. Geometry, not measure theory.

When to Use

Any conditional question about the DGFF (or any Gaussian vector). Specializations I use:

  • \(Y\) = field on \(D \setminus U\) ⇒ domain Markov property: mean = harmonic extension, covariance = \(G_U\) — Use Gaussian conditioning as Markov property.
  • \(Y = \varphi_v\) (one point) ⇒ the puncture identity: conditional covariance \(G_D(x,y) - \frac{G_D(x,v)G_D(v,y)}{G_D(v,v)}\) — the opening move of DGFF on punctured box.
  • Iterated over dyadic scales ⇒ Multiscale decomposition.

What It Converts

Conditional probability (a measure-theoretic operation) into linear algebra (Schur complements). Note the conditional covariance is the Schur complement of \(\Sigma_{YY}\) — conditioning and matrix elimination are the same operation.

Proof Sketch

Write \(X = \Sigma_{XY}\Sigma_{YY}^{-1}Y + Z\); check \(\mathrm{Cov}(Z, Y) = 0\); for jointly Gaussian vectors uncorrelated = independent; read off the law of \(Z\).

Typical Failure Modes

  • Joint Gaussianity is essential — marginally Gaussian with non-Gaussian joint law breaks everything.
  • \(\Sigma_{YY}\) singular: use pseudoinverse / condition on the non-degenerate part.
  • Conditioning on events (e.g. \(\{\varphi_v > 0\}\)) is NOT this formula — only conditioning on linear functionals keeps the magic.
