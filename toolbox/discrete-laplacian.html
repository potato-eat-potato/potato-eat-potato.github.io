Object: Discrete Laplacian

One-Sentence Summary

The operator that says “compare a function to the average of its neighbors” — the grammar in which random walks, harmonic functions, networks, and the DGFF all speak.

Definition and MY CONVENTIONS

My notes use the Laplacian in three normalizations, each natural in one corner of the toolbox. Keeping them straight is my single most likely source of constant errors, so they live here explicitly. They share the same kernel — harmonic (\(\Delta h = 0\)) means the same thing in all three.

Probabilistic (\(\Delta = P - I\)) — the SRW / potential-theory side (Simple random walk on Z2, Potential kernel): \[\Delta f(x) = (P-I)f(x) = \frac{1}{2d}\sum_{y \sim x}\big(f(y) - f(x)\big).\] The generator of the walk; this is the normalization that makes \(\Delta a = \delta_0\) with \(a(e_1) = 1\) for the Potential kernel.

Network — my Random Walks and Electrical Networks notes (Def. 2.2), the electrical / Zeitouni side: \[\Delta f(x) = \sum_{y \sim x} c_{xy}\big(f(y) - f(x)\big),\] unnormalized and conductance-weighted (\(2d\times\) the probabilistic one on unit-conductance \(\mathbb{Z}^d\)); harmonicity and voltages live here, and quantitative formulas carry the total conductance \(c_a\) explicitly.

Dirichlet / killed Laplacian (\(L = I - P = -(P-I)\)) — my DGFF notes, the Gaussian / Bourgade side: symmetric, strictly positive-definite, with inverse the Green function (\(G = L^{-1}\)) and precision matrix of the DGFF.

Relations (unit conductance on \(\mathbb{Z}^d\)): \(\;\Delta_{\text{network}} = 2d\,(P-I) = -2d\,L\). The factor \(2d\) and the sign are what propagate into Green function and the DGFF density, so I convert deliberately when crossing sides.

Intuition

\(\Delta f(x) > 0\): \(f\) at \(x\) sits below its neighborhood average — the walk “drifts uphill in expectation.” Harmonic (\(\Delta f = 0\)) = mean-value property = fair game for the walk = no net current.

Equivalent Interpretations

  • Generator of the SRW: \(\mathbb{E}_x[f(X_1)] - f(x)\).
  • Negative gradient of Dirichlet energy: critical points of \(\mathcal{E}\) with fixed boundary are harmonic.
  • Kirchhoff’s node law for the network with potential \(f\).

Why This Object Is Central to My Probability Route

Every object in my toolbox is defined through it: harmonic functions (\(\Delta h = 0\)), Green function (\(G = L^{-1}\), i.e. \(LG = \delta\)), potential kernel (\(\Delta a = \delta_0\)), DGFF (Gaussian with precision \(L\), covariance \(L^{-1}\)). It is the root node of the whole dependency graph.

Tools That Use This Object

  • Use Green function as inverse Laplacian
  • Turn hitting probability into harmonic function
  • Express DGFF covariance by Green function
