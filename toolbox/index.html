Probability Toolbox

A working toolbox for probability research — notes organized not by textbook topic but by reusable moves: ways to recognize a structure, choose a tool, or attack a problem. It grows out of my reading and talks on random walks, electrical networks, Green functions, and the discrete Gaussian free field. It is deliberately a living document: some notes are polished, others are still developing. The organizing belief is simple — knowledge you can reuse is worth writing down as an action.
