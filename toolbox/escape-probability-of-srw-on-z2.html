Problem

Problem: Escape probability of SRW on Z2

Problem Statement

Estimate \(p_n := \mathbb{P}_0\big(\tau_{\partial B_n} < \tau_0^+\big)\) — the probability SRW reaches distance \(n\) before returning to its start.

Answer: \(p_n = \dfrac{\pi}{2\log n}\,(1 + o(1))\); in particular \(p_n \to 0\): recurrence (Pólya).

Why This Matters

The quantitative heart of two-dimensionality. The walk does return, but so slowly that it almost doesn’t — this “\(1/\log\)” is the same logarithm that makes the 2D DGFF log-correlated, so this problem is secretly the entrance to my entire field-side program.

Solution Routes (in increasing sharpness)

  1. Order of magnitude, electrically: \(p_n = \big(4 R_{\mathrm{eff}}(0, \partial B_n)\big)^{-1}\); short annuli for \(R \gtrsim \log n\) (Use Rayleigh monotonicity by shorting or cutting), test the discrete \(\log\) potential for \(R \lesssim \log n\) (Dirichlet principle). Gives \(p_n \asymp 1/\log n\).
  2. Sharp constant, via potential kernel: optional stopping of \(a(X_k)\) gives \(\mathbb{P}_{e_1}(\tau_{\partial B_n} < \tau_0) = \frac{a(e_1)}{\frac{2}{\pi}\log n + \kappa + o(1)}\) with \(a(e_1) = 1\); first-step decomposition converts to \(p_n\). (Replace divergent Green function by potential kernel.) Equivalent statement: \(R_{\mathrm{eff}}(0,\partial B_n) = \frac{\log n}{2\pi} + O(1)\).

Objects Involved

Simple random walk on Z2, Effective resistance, Potential kernel, Green function

What I Understand

Both routes, including why the electrically lazy route loses the constant and the potential-kernel route keeps it. This was the technical core of my HRUMC talk’s first half.

What Remains Unclear

  • The \(o(1)\): how does it depend on the shape of the domain (box vs. ball vs. irregular)? Connects to the boundary-average error in Replace divergent Green function by potential kernel.

Possible Generalizations

  • Escape from \(x \ne 0\) (inhomogeneous version) — done by the same martingale.
  • Escape probabilities in the punctured or holed box — feeds directly into Random walk on box with mesoscopic hole.
