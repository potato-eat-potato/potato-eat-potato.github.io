Proof Pattern: Finite volume approximation

One-Sentence Summary

Prove it in a box with explicit \(n\)-dependence; let the box grow; what survives is the theorem.

When This Pattern Appears

Whenever the natural object lives on an infinite lattice but is only well-defined (or only computable) after killing at a finite boundary.

General Structure

  1. Replace \(\mathbb{Z}^d\) by \(B_n\); all operators become finite matrices, all sums finite.
  2. Prove the identity/estimate in \(B_n\), tracking constants as explicit functions of \(n\).
  3. Identify the limit type:
    1. monotone (resistances, Green functions — use Compare domains by monotonicity);
    2. after renormalization (differences, gradients — e.g. \(G_{B_n}(0,0) - G_{B_n}(x,0) \to a(x)\), which is how I think the Potential kernel should be defined);
    3. no limit — the \(n\)-dependence is the result (2D DGFF: \(\mathrm{Var} = \frac{2}{\pi}\log n\)).
  4. Control boundary sensitivity: show the bulk quantity changes by \(O(1)\) (or \(o(\text{main term})\)) under change of boundary condition.

Key Estimate or Mechanism

Step 4 is the real content. Standard mechanism: the difference of two boundary conditions is harmonic in the bulk, hence controlled by harmonic measure of the boundary from the bulk point — Boundary-to-bulk comparison.

Typical Inputs

A killed object (Killed Green function); a monotonicity or a renormalization scheme.

Typical Output

Well-posed infinite-volume statements, or honest statements of non-existence with the correct divergence rate.

Common Mistakes or Failure Modes

  • Treating the divergent part as error (in \(d=2\) the \(\log n\) usually IS the answer).
  • Interchanging limits (\(n \to \infty\) vs. some other parameter) without uniformity — the classic.
  • Boundary effects that are \(O(1)\) for bulk points but NOT for points at distance \(o(n)\) from the boundary; state where the estimate holds.

Examples From My Learning

  • Recurrence of \(\mathbb{Z}^2\): \(R_{\mathrm{eff}}(0,\partial B_n) \uparrow \infty\).
  • Escape probability asymptotics (box first, then asymptotics in \(n\)).
  • Everything in the DGFF reading — the field doesn’t exist without this pattern.
