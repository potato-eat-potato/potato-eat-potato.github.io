Object: Simple random walk on Z2

One-Sentence Summary

The nearest-neighbor walk on \(\mathbb{Z}^2\) with uniform steps — the home base of my entire probability route, and the critical dimension where everything acquires logarithms.

Definition

\(X_{n+1} = X_n + \xi_{n+1}\), where \(\xi_i\) are i.i.d. uniform on \(\{\pm e_1, \pm e_2\}\). Transition operator \(Pf(x) = \frac{1}{4}\sum_{y \sim x} f(y)\).

Why \(d = 2\) Is Special

  • Recurrent, but barely (Pólya): the walk returns to \(0\), but the expected return time is infinite, and escape from a box of radius \(n\) before returning happens with probability \(\sim \pi/(2\log n)\). See Escape probability of SRW on Z2.
  • The Green function diverges, so I must use the Potential kernel instead — the move Replace divergent Green function by potential kernel exists because of this dimension.
  • The DGFF built on \(\mathbb{Z}^2\) is log-correlated: variance in a box of side \(N\) is \(\frac{2}{\pi}\log N + O(1)\). Two dimensions is exactly where the field is critical.
  • Almost every interesting quantity (resistance growth, escape probability, field variance) is logarithmic. The logs are the signature of criticality, not a nuisance.

Equivalent Interpretations

  • A martingale in each coordinate (each coordinate is a lazy SRW on \(\mathbb{Z}\)).
  • The Markov chain associated to the electrical network on \(\mathbb{Z}^2\) with unit conductances — see Turn random walk into electrical network.
  • The discrete analogue of 2D Brownian motion; many estimates are discretizations of conformal/logarithmic potential theory.

Common Normalizations or Conventions

Vault convention (recorded in Discrete Laplacian): \(\Delta = P - I\), i.e. \(\Delta f(x) = \frac{1}{4}\sum_{y\sim x}(f(y)-f(x))\). Watch for sources using the unnormalized graph Laplacian (factor \(2d = 4\)).
