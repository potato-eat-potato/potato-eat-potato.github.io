Object: Effective resistance

One-Sentence Summary

\(R_{\mathrm{eff}}(a,z)\) is the single number a network shows the outside world between two terminals — and it is exactly the quantity that converts geometry into random-walk behavior.

Definition

Impose voltage to send unit current from \(a\) to \(z\); then \(R_{\mathrm{eff}}(a,z) = v(a) - v(z)\). Equivalently, via the variational characterizations: \[R_{\mathrm{eff}}(a,z) = \inf\{\mathcal{E}(\theta): \theta \text{ unit flow } a \to z\} = \Big(\inf\{\mathcal{E}(f): f(a)=1,\, f(z)=0\}\Big)^{-1}.\]

The two infima are the variational vise: flows give upper bounds on \(R_{\mathrm{eff}}\) (Thomson principle), test potentials give lower bounds (Dirichlet principle). Almost every resistance estimate I know is one jaw of this vise.

Probabilistic Meaning

  • Escape probability: \(\mathbb{P}_a(\tau_z < \tau_a^+) = \dfrac{1}{c_a\,R_{\mathrm{eff}}(a,z)}\).
  • Recurrence criterion: SRW is recurrent iff \(R_{\mathrm{eff}}(0,\infty) = \infty\).
  • Commute time identity: \(\mathbb{E}_a\tau_z + \mathbb{E}_z\tau_a = 2|E|\,R_{\mathrm{eff}}(a,z)\) (unit conductances).
  • Green function: \(G_D(x,x) = c_xR_{\mathrm{eff}}(x, D^c)\) — see Green function.

Important Properties

  • Metric-like: \(R_{\mathrm{eff}}\) is a metric on the network (triangle inequality holds).
  • Monotone: increasing edge resistances increases \(R_{\mathrm{eff}}\) (Rayleigh monotonicity).
  • On \(\mathbb{Z}^2\): \(R_{\mathrm{eff}}(0, \partial B_n) = \frac{\log n}{2\pi} + O(1)\) — logarithmic growth ⇒ recurrence, but slowly ⇒ “almost transient.”

Why This Object Is Central to My Probability Route

The Zeitouni project taught me that effective resistance is not circuit trivia: it is a probability estimate in disguise. My exposition Exposition - Effective Resistance as a Probability Tool exists to make that case in public.

Tools That Use This Object

  • Use effective resistance to control random walk
  • Use Thomson principle by constructing flow
  • Use Rayleigh monotonicity by shorting or cutting

Common Normalizations or Conventions

Unit conductances unless stated. Watch whether a source’s escape probability formula has \(c_a\) or \(\deg(a)\) — same thing here, not on weighted graphs.

Where I Encountered This
