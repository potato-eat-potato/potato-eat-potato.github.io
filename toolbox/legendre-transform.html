Object: Legendre transform

One-Sentence Summary

\(\Lambda^*(a) = \sup_\lambda\,[\lambda a - \Lambda(\lambda)]\) — the dictionary between “tilting strength” and “target value,” and the shape of every large deviation rate I use.

Definition

For \(\Lambda\) convex (here: \(\Lambda(\lambda) = \log \mathbb{E}\,e^{\lambda X}\), the log-MGF), the transform \(\Lambda^*(a) = \sup_{\lambda}[\lambda a - \Lambda(\lambda)]\).

Intuition

  • Geometric: \(\Lambda^*\) records, for each slope… no — for each point \(a\), how far the line of slope-\(a\)-through-origin can be pushed above \(\Lambda\). Convex duality: points of \(\Lambda^*\) ↔︎ tangent slopes of \(\Lambda\).
  • Probabilistic: the optimizing \(\lambda^*(a)\) is the exponential tilt that recenters the walk at mean \(a\); \(\Lambda^*(a)\) is the relative entropy cost of that tilt. The sup is not formal — it is “choose the cheapest change of measure.” See Change of measure.

Worked Microexample (memorize)

\(X \sim N(0, \sigma^2)\): \(\Lambda(\lambda) = \frac{\lambda^2\sigma^2}{2}\), so \(\Lambda^*(a) = \frac{a^2}{2\sigma^2}\). Every Gaussian tail bound in the DGFF/BRW world is this one line wearing different clothes.

Important Properties

  • Convex, lower semicontinuous, \(\Lambda^*(\mathbb{E}X) = 0\).
  • Involution on nice convex functions: \((\Lambda^*)^* = \Lambda\).
  • Smoothness of \(\Lambda\) ↔︎ strict convexity of \(\Lambda^*\) (duality of differentiability — relevant when rate functions develop flat pieces).

Why This Object Is Central to My Probability Route

BRW speed: solve \(\Lambda^*(v) = \log m\). Large deviation speed selection, barrier computations, and the entropy–energy tradeoff in extremes all route through this transform. It’s the analytic gadget my probability-side arguments borrow most often.

Tools That Use This Object

  • Use Cramer transform for large deviation speed
  • Use first moment method for maximum upper bound (where the exponential count meets the exponential cost)

Where I Encountered This

Large deviations material in my probability preparation; operationally in BRW speed calculations.

Open Confusions

  • Conditions for the involution to hold exactly (Fenchel–Moreau details) — fine for my current uses, should pin down once.
