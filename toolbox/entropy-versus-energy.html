Proof Pattern: Entropy versus energy

One-Sentence Summary

When exponentially many candidates each succeed with exponentially small probability, the answer lives where \(\;\text{(growth rate of candidates)} = \text{(decay rate of success)}\;\) — find the crossing, then justify both sides.

When This Pattern Appears

Extremes of random fields and BRW; speed/front selection; free energy computations; threshold phenomena; basically every “how far does the unlikely-but-numerous reach?” question in my world.

General Structure

  1. Parametrize the target level: \(m = \alpha \cdot (\text{natural scale})\).
  2. Entropy count: \(\log \#(\text{candidates at level } m)\) — particles, sites, paths.
  3. Energy cost: \(-\log \mathbb{P}(\text{one candidate succeeds})\) — a Gaussian exponent or a Legendre transform rate.
  4. Solve entropy \(=\) energy for \(\alpha^*\).
  5. Upper bound at \(\alpha^* + \varepsilon\) by First moment upper bound; lower bound at \(\alpha^* - \varepsilon\) by Second moment lower bound — the heuristic only becomes a theorem in this pincer.

Key Estimate or Mechanism

The crossing equation. Canon examples: - BRW speed: \(\log m = \Lambda^*(v)\). - DGFF max: \(2\log N = \frac{m^2}{2 \cdot \frac{2}{\pi}\log N}\) ⇒ \(m^* = 2\sqrt{2/\pi}\,\log N\). - The optimal trajectory is typically linear in scale (constant rate per scale) — when the optimizer wants to be nonlinear, expect new phenomena (entropic repulsion, barrier effects).

Typical Inputs

A count and a one-candidate tail, both at exponential precision.

Typical Output

Leading-order constants for extremes and fronts. (Corrections beyond leading order are where the pattern hands off to barriers and Multiscale decomposition.)

Common Mistakes or Failure Modes

  • Counting candidates that aren’t really independent attempts and stopping at step 4 (heuristic ≠ proof; the lower bound can genuinely fail at the heuristic level for strongly correlated families if truncation isn’t used).
  • Wrong entropy: count effective candidates (e.g. distinct mesoscopic boxes), not raw ones.

Examples From My Learning

  • BRW speed (Zeitouni notes); DGFF leading max; threshold heuristics in Park’s seminar.

Related Tools

  • Use Cramer transform for large deviation speed, Use first moment method for maximum upper bound

Current Understanding

The heuristic is second nature; the “pincer” justifications are at the same stage as my second-moment truncation skills.
