Proof Pattern: First moment upper bound

One-Sentence Summary

Bound the probability of existence by the expected count: \(\mathbb{P}(Z \ge 1) \le \mathbb{E}[Z]\), then show \(\mathbb{E}[Z] \to 0\).

When This Pattern Appears

Maxima of large families, existence of structures in random objects, “no particle/site/subgraph does X” statements. The dependence structure is irrelevant — linearity of expectation doesn’t care.

General Structure

  1. Encode the event as \(\{Z \ge 1\}\) for a counting variable \(Z = \sum_i \mathbf{1}_{A_i}\).
  2. Compute \(\mathbb{E}[Z] = \sum_i \mathbb{P}(A_i)\) — for BRW use many-to-one; for the DGFF use Gaussian tails with \(\mathrm{Var} = \frac{2}{\pi}\log N\).
  3. Choose the threshold so \(\mathbb{E}[Z] \to 0\); Markov finishes.

Key Estimate or Mechanism

The crossing point of (number of candidates) × (tail of one candidate): solve \(N e^{-\Lambda^*} = 1\). The pattern is sharp at exponential scale exactly when this entropy–energy balance is the true mechanism — see Entropy versus energy.

Typical Inputs

Marginal tail bounds; a count of candidates. Nothing about correlations.

Typical Output

\(\max \le m^* + \varepsilon\) with high probability; nonexistence thresholds.

Common Mistakes or Failure Modes

  • It cannot prove existence. \(\mathbb{E}[Z] \to \infty\) proves nothing without Second moment lower bound.
  • For log-correlated families the naive threshold overshoots at second order (true max is a \(\log\) lower: descendants of early-high particles die — fixing this requires barrier/truncation ideas).
  • Summing a tail bound that isn’t uniform over the family (boundary sites of the DGFF have smaller variance — sometimes helps, sometimes invalidates the uniform bound; check).

Examples From My Learning

  • BRW speed upper bound: \(\mathbb{E}[\#\{|v|=n : S_v > vn\}] = m^n\mathbb{P}(S_n > vn)\) → \(0\) iff \(\Lambda^*(v) > \log m\).
  • DGFF max upper bound at \(2\sqrt{g}\log N\).
  • Threshold computations in Park’s seminar (subgraph counts).
