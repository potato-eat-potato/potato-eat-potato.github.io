Courant Seminar Talk - Lattice Random Walks and Electrical Networks

Two-hour blackboard talk on lattice random walks, electrical networks, and the surrounding probability — Courant seminar context (delivered).

What I Was Trying to Explain

The full technical story that the HRUMC talk could only sketch: with two hours and a blackboard, actual proofs — the harmonicity argument, the variational vise with both jaws proved (Thomson principle via the Pythagoras decomposition), Pólya in both directions, and the worked gem Effective resistance between adjacent vertices in Z2 by superposition.

What Two Hours on a Blackboard Taught Me (vs. 15 minutes of slides)

  • Proof-level exposition exposes every gap: places I “understood” from reading collapsed at the board and had to be rebuilt (the node-law check in flow constructions; the direction of Rayleigh).
  • A blackboard talk is a forcing function for owning material — this talk, more than any reading, is why the Zeitouni-side tools in this vault are marked usable rather than understood.

Which Tools Were Central

Turn random walk into electrical network · Use Thomson principle by constructing flow · Use Rayleigh monotonicity by shorting or cutting · Energy minimization argument
