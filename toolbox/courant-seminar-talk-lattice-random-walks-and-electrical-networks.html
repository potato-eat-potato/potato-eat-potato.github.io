Courant Seminar Talk - Lattice Random Walks and Electrical Networks

Two-hour blackboard talk on lattice random walks, electrical networks, and the surrounding probability — Courant seminar context (delivered). Written up in full as Random Walks and Electrical Networks: Harmonic Functions, Voltage, Effective Resistance, and Rayleigh–Thomson Theory (17 pp).

The Written Notes (contents)

The 17-page write-up is the self-contained version of this talk — the closest thing in the vault to a single document that generates the whole Zeitouni-side toolbox:

  1. Networks — weighted graphs, conductances, the induced random walk (Simple random walk on Z2 is the unit-conductance case).
  2. Harmonic functions and the weighted graph Laplacian (Discrete Laplacian).
  3. Maximum principle and uniqueness for the Dirichlet problem (Maximum principle).
  4. Hitting probabilities are harmonic (Turn hitting probability into harmonic function).
  5. Voltage and current: voltage \(=\) hitting probability (Turn random walk into electrical network).
  6. Escape probability and effective resistance, including the \(c_a\)-factor subtlety (Effective resistance).
  7. Unit flow; 8. conservation of energy and the cycle law. 9.–11. Thomson’s principle (Thomson principle) and Rayleigh monotonicity (Rayleigh monotonicity) — the variational vise.
  8. The dictionary, summarized; 13. what comes next.

What I Was Trying to Explain

The full technical story that the HRUMC talk could only sketch: with two hours and a blackboard, actual proofs — the harmonicity argument, the variational vise with both jaws proved (Thomson principle via the Pythagoras decomposition), Pólya in both directions, and the worked gem Effective resistance between adjacent vertices in Z2 by superposition.

What Two Hours on a Blackboard Taught Me (vs. 15 minutes of slides)

  • Proof-level exposition exposes every gap: places I “understood” from reading collapsed at the board and had to be rebuilt (the node-law check in flow constructions; the direction of Rayleigh).
  • A blackboard talk is a forcing function for owning material — this talk, more than any reading, is why the Zeitouni-side tools in this vault are marked usable rather than understood.

Which Tools Were Central
