Proof Pattern: Change of measure

One-Sentence Summary

Make the rare event typical under a new measure, do the easy computation there, and pay the Radon–Nikodym price on the way back.

When This Pattern Appears

Large deviation lower bounds; spine/size-biasing arguments for branching processes; Gaussian shift arguments (Cameron–Martin); any “condition on the unlikely and describe what it looks like” question.

General Structure

  1. Choose the tilt: a new measure \(\mathbb{Q}\) under which the target event \(A\) has probability \(\approx 1\).
  2. Write \(\mathbb{P}(A) = \mathbb{E}_\mathbb{Q}\big[\frac{d\mathbb{P}}{d\mathbb{Q}}\mathbf{1}_A\big]\).
  3. Control the density on the event — ideally \(\frac{d\mathbb{P}}{d\mathbb{Q}} \approx e^{-(\text{cost})}\) is nearly constant on \(A\).
  4. Conclude \(\mathbb{P}(A) \approx e^{-\text{cost}} \cdot \mathbb{Q}(A) \approx e^{-\text{cost}}\).

Key Estimate or Mechanism

The cost is a relative entropy \(H(\mathbb{Q}\,|\,\mathbb{P})\), and choosing \(\mathbb{Q}\) = minimizing entropy subject to realizing the event is exactly the Legendre transform optimization. My three standard tilts:

  • Exponential tilt of a walk (\(d\mathbb{Q} \propto e^{\lambda S_n}d\mathbb{P}\)): cost \(n\Lambda^*(a)\) — Cramér’s lower bound.
  • Gaussian mean shift (shift DGFF by deterministic \(h\)): cost \(\frac{1}{2}\mathcal{E}(h)\) — Dirichlet energy is the large-deviation price of reshaping the field (Cameron–Martin). The bridge between my energy world and my LD world.
  • Spine / size-biasing for BRW: tilt by an additive martingale; under \(\mathbb{Q}\) one distinguished particle does the rare thing — the modern engine for BRW fine results (statement-level knowledge for now).

Typical Inputs

An explicit density; a candidate optimal profile (from the entropy-energy heuristic).

Typical Output

Sharp lower bounds matching Chernoff upper bounds; structural descriptions of conditioned processes (“what does the field look like given a high point” = shifted field).

Common Mistakes or Failure Modes

  • Density not nearly-constant on \(A\) (then step 3 needs a second localization — restrict \(A\) further).
  • Tilting “the wrong coordinate” (tilt the increment process, not the endpoint).
  • For Gaussian shifts: factor-of-2 and normalization errors in the cost — recompute \(\frac12\mathcal{E}(h)\) in vault conventions once and record it HERE (open TODO).
