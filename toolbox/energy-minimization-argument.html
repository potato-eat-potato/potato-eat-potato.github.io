Proof Pattern: Energy minimization argument

One-Sentence Summary

When the target quantity is the minimum of a quadratic energy, prove bounds by exhibiting competitors — never by solving the minimization.

When This Pattern Appears

Resistance/capacity bounds; transience and recurrence; eigenvalue-type bounds; any quantity with a variational characterization.

General Structure

  1. Identify the variational characterization and, crucially, its direction: is the target an inf over flows (Thomson principle, upper bounds) or an inf over potentials (Dirichlet principle, which upper-bounds conductance, i.e. lower-bounds resistance)?
  2. Steal a competitor from the continuum: the harmonic profile (\(\log|x|\) in 2D, \(|x|^{2-d}\) in higher \(d\)), the radial flow, the product/interpolation profile.
  3. Discretize the competitor; verify admissibility exactly (node law for flows, boundary values for potentials).
  4. Compute its energy in shells: \(\sum_k (\#\text{edges in shell } k) \times (\text{value on shell } k)^2\).
  5. If both directions are needed, build one competitor of each type and compare the constants (the duality gap is zero, so good guesses nearly meet).

Key Estimate or Mechanism

The shell sum. In \(\mathbb{Z}^2\) shells have \(\asymp k\) edges and harmonic profiles have increments \(\asymp 1/k\), giving the universal \(\sum_k k \cdot k^{-2} = \log\) — the same \(\log\) in resistance, escape probability, and DGFF variance. Recognize it on sight.

Typical Inputs

A variational principle; a continuum intuition for the optimizer.

Typical Output

Two-sided order-sharp bounds with explicit constants.

Common Mistakes or Failure Modes

  • Direction confusion (see step 1) — I keep the mnemonic “flows over, potentials under” in Effective resistance.
  • Inadmissible competitor (node law violated at the apex of a radial flow — patch by hand near the source).
  • Computing the energy of the continuum object and forgetting discretization corrections near the singularity.
