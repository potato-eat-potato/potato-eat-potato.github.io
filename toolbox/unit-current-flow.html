Object: Unit current flow

One-Sentence Summary

The physically realized flow of one ampere from \(a\) to \(z\) — among all unit flows, the unique one of minimal energy, and the one the random walk itself traces out.

Definition

A unit flow \(\theta\) from \(a\) to \(z\): antisymmetric edge function with Kirchhoff’s node law (\(\mathrm{div}\,\theta = 0\) off \(\{a,z\}\)) and net flux \(1\) out of \(a\). The unit current flow \(i\) is the one induced by the harmonic voltage via Ohm’s law: \(i(x,y) = c_{xy}(v(x) - v(y))\).

Equivalent Interpretations

  • Minimizer of energy \(\mathcal{E}(\theta) = \sum_e \theta(e)^2 r_e\) over all unit flows; \(\mathcal{E}(i) = R_{\mathrm{eff}}(a,z)\) — Thomson principle.
  • Probabilistic: \(i(x,y)\) = expected net number of signed traversals of edge \((x,y)\) by the walk from \(a\) before hitting \(z\) (Doyle–Snell). Current is a random-walk statistic, not just physics.

Why It Matters

Flows are the constructible side of the variational vise: I can write one down by hand (mimic the continuum radial flow, push mass along a tree, spread along shells) and instantly get \(R_{\mathrm{eff}} \le \mathcal{E}(\theta)\). The current flow is the certificate that the bound is tight.

Tools That Use This Object

  • Use Thomson principle by constructing flow
  • Use effective resistance to control random walk

Problems Where This Appears

  • Effective resistance between adjacent vertices in Z2 — the superposition trick: unit current \(0 \to e_1\) = (flow out of \(0\)) + (flow into \(e_1\)), each spreading \(1/4\) per incident edge by symmetry, so the direct edge carries \(\tfrac14 + \tfrac14 = \tfrac12\), giving \(R_{\mathrm{eff}} = \tfrac12\).
  • Transience of \(\mathbb{Z}^3\): radial-ish flow with energy \(\sum_k k^2 \cdot (k^{-2})^2 = \sum k^{-2} < \infty\).

Where I Encountered This

Doyle–Snell with Zeitouni; the superposition computation was a highlight of my two-hour Courant blackboard talk.
