Proof Pattern: Multiscale decomposition

One-Sentence Summary

Decompose a long-range-correlated object into a telescoping sum over geometric scales, prove a uniform estimate per scale, and recombine with a union or martingale argument.

When This Pattern Appears

Log-correlated fields, hierarchical models, anything whose covariance is a sum of scale contributions; also classical multiscale analysis of walks (coarse-graining trajectories by dyadic boxes).

General Structure

  1. Fix the hierarchy: dyadic boxes \(B^{(k)}\) of side \(N 2^{-k}\), \(k = 0, \dots, \log_2 N\).
  2. Decompose: \(\varphi = \sum_k W_k\) with \(W_k\) = (conditional expectation given scale-\(k\) boundary data) increments — each split is one application of Use Gaussian conditioning as Markov property.
  3. Per-scale estimate, uniform in \(k\): each \(W_k\) has variance \(\approx \frac{2}{\pi}\log 2\), independence across boxes at the same scale.
  4. Recombine: treat \((W_1(x), W_1(x)+W_2(x), \dots)\) as a tree-indexed walk and import Branching random walk technology (barriers, first/second moments along the tree).
  5. Pay the approximation tax: the increments are only nearly independent/identical; isolate and bound the defect (this step is the hard, paper-specific part).

Key Estimate or Mechanism

“Variance budget per scale is \(O(1)\), number of scales is \(\log N\)” — fine behavior is decided by how the \(O(1)\) pieces cooperate across scales, which is a BRW question.

Typical Inputs

A Markov/Gibbs–Markov property to generate the decomposition; per-scale Green function asymptotics.

Typical Output

Sharp extremes (\(2\sqrt g \log N - \frac34\sqrt g\log\log N\) for the 2D DGFF), barrier probabilities, structure of near-maxima.

Common Mistakes or Failure Modes

  • Pretending exact BRW structure (the defect in step 5 can eat the \(\log\log\)-order terms if ignored — they are exactly the precision at stake).
  • Hierarchies not aligned with the question (the right tree for the max near a point is centered at that point).
