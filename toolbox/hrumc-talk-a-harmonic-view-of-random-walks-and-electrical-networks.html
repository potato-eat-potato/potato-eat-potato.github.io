HRUMC Talk — A Harmonic View of Random Walks and Electrical Networks

“A Harmonic View of Random Walks and Electrical Networks: From Dirichlet Problems to Recurrence of \(\mathbb{Z}^2\)” Hudson River Undergraduate Mathematics Conference — April 25, 2026 (delivered). My speaker prep note for this talk lives in the vault; the same material, written up in full, is the Random Walks and Electrical Networks notes.

One-Line Thesis (the slide-2 spine)

harmonicity \(\Rightarrow\) uniqueness \(\Rightarrow\) random walk / electrical dictionary \(\Rightarrow\) resistance estimate.

The whole talk is built through harmonic functions: one local averaging law that both the probabilistic and the electrical side obey.

The Arc (as delivered)

  1. Discrete harmonicity is a local averaging law. \(f\) is harmonic at \(x\) iff \(f(x)\) equals the average of its neighbours — and that is exactly the one-step expectation for Simple random walk on Z2. (Turn hitting probability into harmonic function.)
  2. Random-walk side. Hitting probabilities solve a Dirichlet problem.
  3. Electrical side. Voltages solve the same Dirichlet problem, with the same boundary data.
  4. Uniqueness principle. Two harmonic functions agreeing on the boundary agree everywhere (Maximum principle); hence probabilistic questions \(\Leftrightarrow\) electrical quantities — the dictionary. (Turn random walk into electrical network.)
  5. From recurrence to resistance. A return problem becomes a deterministic estimate of \(R_{\mathrm{eff}}(0 \leftrightarrow \infty)\): recurrence \(\Leftrightarrow R_{\mathrm{eff}}(0,\infty) = \infty\).
  6. Surgery, then the finale. Shorting and cutting laws (Use Rayleigh monotonicity by shorting or cutting); then recurrence of \(\mathbb{Z}^2\): shorting the annuli reduces \(R_{\mathrm{eff}}(0,\partial B_n)\) to a constant multiple of the harmonic series, which diverges. (Rayleigh monotonicity.)

Which Tools Were Central

Turn hitting probability into harmonic function · Turn random walk into electrical network · Use Rayleigh monotonicity by shorting or cutting

What This Talk Reveals About My Interests

Connections-first: I chose to show that two theories are secretly one (through harmonic functions) rather than to prove a single hard theorem. Under a time limit I kept the concrete payoff — recurrence of \(\mathbb{Z}^2\) falling out of a harmonic series — and dropped generality.

What the Talk Did Not Cover (and where it points)

The talk stops at recurrence of \(\mathbb{Z}^2\). It does not reach the Green function as a covariance or the DGFF — that is the natural sequel, and exactly the direction of my Bourgade reading. The planned write-up Exposition - Why Green Functions Connect Random Walks and DGFF continues the story past where the talk ended.
