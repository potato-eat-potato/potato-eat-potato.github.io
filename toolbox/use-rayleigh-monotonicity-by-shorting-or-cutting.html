Tool

Tool: Use Rayleigh monotonicity by shorting or cutting

One-Sentence Summary

Surgery instead of computation: collapse vertices (short) to lower-bound resistance, delete edges (cut) to upper-bound it, then compute the mutilated network exactly.

The Mnemonic

Short for lower bounds, cut for upper bounds.

  • Shorting (gluing vertices) only decreases \(R_{\mathrm{eff}}\): \(R \ge R_{\text{shorted}}\).
  • Cutting (deleting edges) only increases \(R_{\mathrm{eff}}\): \(R \le R_{\text{cut}}\).

Both are instances of Rayleigh monotonicity (conductance \(\to \infty\) resp. \(\to 0\)).

What Problem Does This Solve?

Resistance bounds on networks too irregular to solve, by comparing to series/parallel circuits or trees that I can solve.

Input

A network and a vision of which identifications/deletions produce a computable circuit.

Output

A one-sided bound on \(R_{\mathrm{eff}}\), hence on escape/recurrence via Use effective resistance to control random walk.

Typical Use Cases

  • Recurrence of \(\mathbb{Z}^2\): short each annulus \(\partial B_k\) to a point; the result is a series circuit of parallel edge bundles, \(R_{\mathrm{eff}}(0,\partial B_n) \ge \sum_{k<n} \frac{1}{\#\{\text{edges } k \to k+1\}} \asymp \sum \frac{1}{8k} \asymp \log n \to \infty\). (This is Nash–Williams in disguise.)
  • Transience of \(\mathbb{Z}^3\) via subnetworks: cutting down to a transient subtree shows \(R_{\mathbb{Z}^3} \le R_{\text{tree}} < \infty\) — “a transient subgraph makes the whole graph transient.”
  • Robustness: adding edges/obstacles to a recurrent graph — what survives? First question: which direction does monotonicity point?

What It Converts

  • from: an irregular network
  • to: a series/parallel circuit or tree with an exact answer

When Should I Think of This?

  • Before any computation: can surgery + a back-of-envelope circuit already settle the order?
  • When perturbing a known graph (holes, extra edges): monotonicity often gives one direction for free — relevant to Random walk on box with mesoscopic hole.

Where This Appears in My Path

The shorted-annuli proof of \(\mathbb{Z}^2\) recurrence was the emotional high point of my HRUMC talk — recurrence of a lattice from a harmonic series.

Current Understanding

Solid. The discipline is remembering which surgery gives which direction (hence the mnemonic).

Open Confusions
