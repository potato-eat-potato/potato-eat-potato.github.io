HRUMC Talk - Random Walks Electrical Networks and DGFF

“Connections Between Random Walks, Electrical Networks, and the Discrete Gaussian Free Field” Hudson River Undergraduate Mathematics Conference — April 25, 2026 (delivered).

What I Was Trying to Explain

That three apparently different theories are one theory: the random walk, the resistor network, and the DGFF all answer to the same operator (\(-\Delta\)), and the Green function is the rosetta stone. Arc of the talk:

  1. Hitting probabilities are harmonic → Dirichlet problem (Turn hitting probability into harmonic function).
  2. Same Dirichlet problem = voltages → the dictionary, recurrence of \(\mathbb{Z}^2\) by shorting (Use Rayleigh monotonicity by shorting or cutting).
  3. Invert the Laplacian → Green function; visits = resistance via \(G_D(x,x) = c(x)R_{\mathrm{eff}}\).
  4. A positive-definite kernel is a covariance → the DGFF, log-correlations as the shadow of \(R_{\mathrm{eff}} \sim \log\).

Which Tools Were Central

Turn random walk into electrical network · Use Green function as inverse Laplacian · Express DGFF covariance by Green function

What This Talk Reveals About My Interests

I care about identities that fuse theories more than about any single theory; and my instinct under a time limit is to sacrifice generality, keep exact constants (\(\pi/(2\log n)\) stayed in; generic graphs went out).
