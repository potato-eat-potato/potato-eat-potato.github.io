Proof Pattern: Second moment lower bound

One-Sentence Summary

Existence with positive probability from \(\mathbb{P}(Z > 0) \ge (\mathbb{E}Z)^2 / \mathbb{E}[Z^2]\) — works exactly when pairs are nearly uncorrelated, and is rescued by truncation when they are not.

When This Pattern Appears

Matching lower bounds after a first moment upper bound: maxima are achieved, structures exist, thresholds are sharp.

General Structure

  1. Same count \(Z\) as in First moment upper bound (possibly restricted — see step 4).
  2. Expand \(\mathbb{E}[Z^2] = \sum_{i,j} \mathbb{P}(A_i \cap A_j)\); stratify pairs by correlation (BRW: generation of the common ancestor; DGFF: dyadic distance \(|x-y| \approx N^{1-k/\log N}\)… i.e. number of shared scales).
  3. Show the diagonal/decorrelated strata dominate: \(\mathbb{E}[Z^2] \le C(\mathbb{E}Z)^2\). Paley–Zygmund gives \(\mathbb{P}(Z>0) \ge 1/C\).
  4. If step 3 fails (it does, near the true max of BRW/DGFF — heavily correlated pairs blow up the sum): redefine \(Z\) to count only particles whose entire trajectory across scales stays below a barrier. First moment barely changes; second moment collapses to the tractable case. (Bramson’s truncation.)
  5. Boost “positive probability” to “with high probability” if needed (zero-one arguments, sprinkling, or concentration).

Key Estimate or Mechanism

The pair-overlap sum: for two particles sharing \(k\) of \(n\) generations/scales, \(\mathbb{P}(\text{both high}) \approx \mathbb{P}(\text{one high})^2 \cdot e^{+(\text{shared-variance gain at } k)}\); summing over \(k\) must converge geometrically. The barrier exists to kill the \(k \approx n\) terms.

Typical Inputs

Pairwise joint laws (two-point function); for step 4, a path/scale decomposition of each variable — this is where Multiscale decomposition is consumed.

Typical Output

Matching lower bound: max \(\ge m^* - \varepsilon\), sharp thresholds.

Common Mistakes or Failure Modes

  • Confusing “positive probability” with “whp” (PZ alone gives the former).
  • Truncating the count but forgetting to re-verify the first moment of the truncated count.
  • Stratification too coarse: the geometric series in \(k\) must actually be exhibited.
