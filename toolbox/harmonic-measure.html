Object: Harmonic measure

One-Sentence Summary

Where the walk exits: \(\mathrm{hm}_D(x, \cdot) = \mathbb{P}_x(X_{\tau_{D^c}} \in \cdot)\) — the probabilistic answer to “which boundary points does the domain actually see from \(x\)?”

Definition

For \(x \in D\) and \(A \subset \partial D\) (outer boundary): \(\mathrm{hm}_D(x, A) = \mathbb{P}_x(X_{\tau} \in A)\), \(\tau\) = exit time of \(D\).

Equivalent Interpretations

  • The exit distribution of the random walk.
  • The representing measure for the Dirichlet problem: \(u(x) = \sum_{y \in \partial D} u(y)\,\mathrm{hm}_D(x, y)\) solves \(\Delta u = 0\) with given boundary data — this is Convert boundary value problem into probabilistic representation.
  • In \(\mathbb{Z}^2\) there is also harmonic measure from infinity for a finite set \(A\) (limit of \(\mathbb{P}_x(\text{first hit of } A \in \cdot)\) as \(x \to \infty\) — exists by recurrence), proportional to the equilibrium-type measure of \(A\).

Why It Matters

Harmonic measure is how geometry enters estimates: protruding parts of the boundary carry more of it, screened parts carry exponentially little. Every “the walk doesn’t notice the far side of the obstacle” argument is a harmonic measure statement.

Why This Object Is Central to My Probability Route

My punctured-box and mesoscopic-hole questions are at heart questions about the harmonic measure of small removed sets: the conditioned field / killed walk feels the hole exactly through it. See Harmonic measure near removed set.

Important Properties

  • \(\mathrm{hm}_D(x,\cdot)\) is harmonic in \(x\) for fixed target set.
  • Carleman/Beurling-type estimates control it near slits and obstacles — flagged as future reading (Lawler’s books).
  • Relation to Equilibrium potential and capacity: hitting probabilities of small sets ≈ (Green factor) × (capacity).

Where I Encountered This

Implicitly throughout Doyle–Snell (exit probabilities); explicitly when formulating the punctured box question.

Open Confusions

  • Quantitative harmonic measure near a mesoscopic removed set in a box: which scales dominate? This is really my research question in disguise.
