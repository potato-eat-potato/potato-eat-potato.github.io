Exposition - Effective Resistance as a Probability Tool

Planned written exposition; second public-website artifact. Distilled from the Courant two-hour talk.

Thesis

Effective resistance deserves to be taught as a probability tool with a physics name: one number that upper- and lower-bounds escape probabilities, decides recurrence, computes commute times — and is itself bounded by a two-jawed variational vise anyone can operate.

Planned Outline

  1. The dictionary, fast (Turn random walk into electrical network).
  2. The vise: flows bound \(R\) above (Thomson principle), potentials below (Dirichlet principle) — with the Pythagoras proof, once, for both.
  3. Surgery: short for lower bounds, cut for upper bounds (Use Rayleigh monotonicity by shorting or cutting).
  4. Three set pieces:
    • Pólya, both dimensions, one page each;
    • Effective resistance between adjacent vertices in Z2 — three proofs of \(\tfrac12\);
    • Escape probability of SRW on Z2 with the sharp constant.
  5. Where this goes next: \(G = cR\) and the road to Gaussian fields (handoff to the Green-function exposition).
