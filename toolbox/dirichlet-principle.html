Theorem

Theorem: Dirichlet principle

Statement

Among all functions with \(f(a) = 1\), \(f(z) = 0\) (more generally, fixed boundary data), the Dirichlet energy is uniquely minimized by the harmonic voltage \(v\), and \[C_{\mathrm{eff}}(a,z) = \frac{1}{R_{\mathrm{eff}}(a,z)} = \min_{f(a)=1,\,f(z)=0} \mathcal{E}(f).\]

Intuition

Harmonic functions are the laziest interpolations of their boundary values. Any test function I write down interpolates more violently, dissipates more, and so certifies \(C_{\mathrm{eff}} \le \mathcal{E}(f)\), i.e. \(R_{\mathrm{eff}} \ge 1/\mathcal{E}(f)\).

When to Use

Whenever I need \(R_{\mathrm{eff}} \ge (\text{something})\): escape probability upper bounds, recurrence proofs, \(G_D(x,x)\) lower bounds. Dual jaw to Thomson principle. The recipe: guess a radially symmetric profile (in \(\mathbb{Z}^2\): \(f \sim \log\)), compute its energy shell by shell.

What It Converts

Resistance lower bounds (hard direction by surgery) into “write down one function” — the continuum potential theory I know (log potentials!) becomes a rigorous discrete input.

Proof Sketch

Same Pythagoras as Thomson, run in the potential space: decompose \(f = v + g\) with \(g = 0\) on terminals; cross term vanishes since \(v\) is harmonic off the terminals (sum by parts); \(\mathcal{E}(f) = \mathcal{E}(v) + \mathcal{E}(g)\).

Typical Failure Modes

  • Wrong normalization of boundary values (the principle computes conductance, not resistance — invert at the end; I have botched this once already).
  • Test function not satisfying the boundary constraints exactly.
  • On infinite graphs: which boundary condition at infinity is being imposed matters (free vs. wired — flag from Lyons–Peres, process later).

Related Tools

  • Use effective resistance to control random walk
  • Use Rayleigh monotonicity by shorting or cutting (often interchangeable; Dirichlet gives better constants, shorting gives faster proofs)

Where I Have Used or Seen This

Zeitouni project: the \(f(x) = \log|x|\)-profile lower bound for \(R_{\mathrm{eff}}(0, \partial B_n)\) in \(\mathbb{Z}^2\), complementing the shorting argument.
