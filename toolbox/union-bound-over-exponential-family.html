Proof Pattern: Union bound over exponential family

One-Sentence Summary

Organize a huge union so the summands decay geometrically; then the whole union costs only a constant times its worst term.

When This Pattern Appears

Uniform bounds over scales, levels, boxes, or times: “no scale misbehaves,” “no dyadic box is bad,” maxima over parametrized families. The quantitative backbone under First moment upper bound whenever the family is structured rather than flat.

General Structure

  1. Stratify the family by a parameter \(k\) (scale, level, distance, generation).
  2. Bound each stratum: \(\mathbb{P}(\text{bad at } k) \le e^{-c\,\phi(k)}\) with \(\phi\) strictly increasing (linear in \(k\) is the usual win).
  3. Sum the geometric series: \(\sum_k e^{-c\phi(k)} \le C e^{-c\phi(k_0)}\) — the first/worst stratum dominates.
  4. Read off: the union is as unlikely as its most likely member, up to constants.

Key Estimate or Mechanism

Designing the stratification so step 2 has uniform constants and genuinely increasing exponents. The skill is choosing the parametrization — by dyadic distance for DGFF pairs, by generation gap for BRW ancestors, by annulus for walk excursions.

Typical Inputs

Exponential one-event bounds (Gaussian tails, Cramér); a hierarchy to stratify along.

Typical Output

“With high probability, simultaneously for all \(k\)…” statements — the uniformity that converts pointwise estimates into path/field statements.

Common Mistakes or Failure Modes

  • Polynomially many terms each barely controlled: if the per-stratum bound doesn’t improve along the family, the union bound just loses a log — sometimes fatal at \(\log\log\) precision (DGFF corrections!).
  • Hidden double counting across strata (fine, but wasteful — only hurts constants).
  • When geometric decay is unobtainable, this pattern is the wrong hammer: chaining/Dudley-type arguments are the next tool up (future reading flag).

  • “No annulus is crossed too often” estimates in random walk arguments (Zeitouni project).
