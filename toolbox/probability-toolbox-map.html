My Mathematics in One Paragraph

I study how randomness interacts with the geometry of \(\mathbb{Z}^2\) through three interlocking theories — random walks, electrical networks, and Gaussian free fields — joined by one object (the Green function) and one quadratic form (Dirichlet energy). The walk side gives me variational tools with sharp constants; the field side gives me log-correlated structure and extremes; branching random walks and large deviations are the machinery that makes the extremes computable. The vault is organized by moves, not topics: see Why I Am Building This Toolbox.

The Spine (connections I navigate by)

  • Random walk → electrical networks, through voltage/current/resistance (Turn random walk into electrical network).
  • Green functions tie walks, inverse Laplacians, and DGFF covariance into one object (Green function); \(G_D(x,x) = c(x)R_{\mathrm{eff}}\) is the master identity.
  • DGFF → random walk, through killed Green functions (Express DGFF covariance by Green function).
  • BRW → DGFF, through extremes and multiscale structure (Use multiscale decomposition).
  • Large deviations → BRW, through speed selection and first moments (Use Cramer transform for large deviation speed).
