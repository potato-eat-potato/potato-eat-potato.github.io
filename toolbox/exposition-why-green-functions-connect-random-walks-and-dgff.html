Exposition - Why Green Functions Connect Random Walks and DGFF

Planned written exposition; primary public-website artifact. Built from the HRUMC talk’s second half, expanded to include what the talk had to skip.

Thesis

The Green function is one object wearing three uniforms — occupation time (probability), inverse Laplacian (analysis), covariance (Gaussian geometry) — and the DGFF is what you get when you take the third uniform seriously. The honest punchline: \(G_D(x,x) = c(x)R_{\mathrm{eff}}(x, D^c)\), so field variance is electrical resistance, and log-correlation of the 2D field is the log-growth of 2D resistance.

Planned Outline

  1. Visits before exit: \(G_D\) as a counting object; geometric-visits identity (Strong Markov property).
  2. The matrix view: \(G_D = (-\Delta_D)^{-1}\); positivity of the inverse as a probabilistic miracle.
  3. Interlude: why \(d=2\) needs the Potential kernel (and why that’s a feature).
  4. Covariances need positive-definiteness; we have it; define the DGFF.
  5. The dictionary’s last column: variance = resistance; log-correlations computed from first principles.
  6. Coda: conditioning = domain surgery (puncture identity) — one teaser computation.
