Tool: Express DGFF covariance by Green function

One-Sentence Summary

Every question about the DGFF is a question about \(G_D\) in a Gaussian costume: translate field statements into Green function estimates, prove them with walk techniques, translate back.

What Problem Does This Solve?

Variances, correlations, conditional laws, and comparison inequalities for the DGFF — none of which are accessible from the density directly.

Input

The defining identity \(\mathbb{E}[\varphi_x \varphi_y] = G_D(x,y)\) (which follows from: density \(\propto e^{-\frac12\langle \varphi, (-\Delta)\varphi\rangle}\) and “covariance of a Gaussian = inverse of the quadratic form” — i.e. Use Green function as inverse Laplacian upgraded to probability).

Output

Field estimates with walk-side proofs. Standard dividends in \(d = 2\), box of side \(N\):

  • Variance in the bulk: \(\mathrm{Var}(\varphi_x) = \frac{2}{\pi}\log N + O(1)\).
  • Log-correlations: \(\mathbb{E}[\varphi_x\varphi_y] \approx \frac{2}{\pi}\log\frac{N}{|x-y|}\).
  • Domain monotonicity of variances (from monotonicity of \(G\)): Compare domains by monotonicity.
  • Gradient field has \(O(1)\) variance even as \(N \to \infty\) (differences of Green functions converge — the Potential kernel shows up).

What It Converts

  • from: Gaussian field questions
  • to: random walk / potential theory estimates (where my Zeitouni-side tools apply)

Core Idea

A centered Gaussian field is its covariance. Since the covariance is \(G_D\), the entire Zeitouni half of my toolbox (resistance, potential kernel, harmonic analysis of the walk) becomes a DGFF toolbox with zero new probability. This is the bridge my whole vault is organized around.

When Should I Think of This?

Always, when the DGFF appears. The first question for any field statement: what does this say about \(G_D\)?

Where This Appears in My Path

The climactic identity of my HRUMC talk, and the daily engine of the Bourgade reading. The punctured box question begins by applying this plus the puncture identity in Killed Green function.
